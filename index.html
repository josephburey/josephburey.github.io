About

Welcome! I am a PhD Candidate in Educational Psychology at the University of Minnesota. My research focuses on students' psychological responses to self-relevant racial identity representations, with a particular emphasis on investigating the influence of critical contexts on Black students' understandings and attributions of systemic racism.

My work sits at the intersection of educational psychology, racial identity development, and critical consciousness, examining how different types of representation impact student experiences and learning outcomes.

Research

My research investigates how different forms of racial identity representation impact students' psychological experiences and learning outcomes. I am particularly interested in understanding how critical contexts influence Black students' interpretations of systemic racism.

Critical Consciousness in Black Education

This project examines issues of misrepresentation and systemic resistance in educational contexts, focusing on how these factors influence the development of critical consciousness among Black students.

AP African American Studies Curriculum Analysis

Using natural language processing techniques, this study investigates recent changes to the AP African American Studies curriculum, analyzing their implications for student learning and representation.

Computer-Based Literacy Learning

Research examining the effectiveness of ELCII on kindergarteners' inference skill development, with a focus on creating inclusive and effective digital learning environments.
